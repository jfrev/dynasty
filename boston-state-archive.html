From FCS to the ACC in Two Years: Inside Boston State's Fast, Expensive Rise

Kingdom Holding Company's investment turned a mid-major into an ACC program with a back-to-back national champion on the sideline. The rest of the league is still adjusting to it.

By Priya Nakamura · The Liberty Ledger

Two years ago, Boston State was playing FCS football in front of a few thousand people. Today, they're an ACC program with a payroll and a facilities budget that rival programs a century older, and the fastest rise of any team in the country has a name behind it: Kingdom Holding Company, the Saudi-based investment firm that bankrolled the jump from FCS to FBS, funded the push into the ACC, and wrote the checks that got both a roster overhaul and Jack Kanoff's signature on a contract.

Kanoff, fresh off back-to-back national championships at Midwestern University, is the headline of the whole operation.

The skepticism from the rest of the league is real and out loud. Kanoff's answer, so far, has been the same one he gave at Midwestern: let the record speak.
