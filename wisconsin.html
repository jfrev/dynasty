Wisconsin Shocks No. 4 Miami, 38-21, in the Biggest Win of Dirk Diggler's Tenure

Three losses in, the Badgers needed a statement. They got the loudest one available, taking down a top-five team by 17.

By Erik Solheim · The Camp Randall Chronicle

Forget everything written about Wisconsin's .500 start through seven weeks — the Badgers just beat a top-five team by three scores. Wisconsin took down No. 4 Miami 38-21, the single biggest win of Dirk Diggler's head coaching career and, by a wide margin, the best result of his first season in Madison.

The formula was exactly what Diggler has preached since he arrived from Auburn: control the line of scrimmage, run the ball until the defense stops respecting anything else, and let a patient offense wear a talented opponent down. It worked as well as it possibly could have — Wisconsin controlled the clock for most of four quarters and put the game away with a physical fourth quarter that mirrored the identity this staff has been selling since August.

On the recruiting side, the 2027 class remains modest — No. 84 nationally, with four-star guard Josh Sambrailo as the lone commitment so far — but a win of this magnitude rarely hurts a coaching staff's pitch to prospects. Diggler's team is 5-3 now, and after a result like this, nobody in the ACC is looking past Wisconsin again anytime soon.
