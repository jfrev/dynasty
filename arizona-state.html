Unbeaten Meets Undefeated: No. 11 Kentucky Comes to Tempe, and a Top-5 Class Arrives With It

Arizona State is 6-1, hosting an undefeated Kentucky team, and quietly building the No. 5 recruiting class in the country.

By Nico Alvarado · The Desert Fork Report

Slammin Beers wanted this game the moment the schedule came out. A first-time head coach with a 6-1 record and one league loss doesn't get many chances to make a signature statement, and hosting an undefeated, No. 11 Kentucky team in Tempe is about as loud a stage as this program gets this year.

The Sun Devils have won with the same formula all year — a physical run game and a secondary that's finally deep enough to match speed with speed — and Beers has stuck to it even after the one loss earlier this season. The recruiting trail has followed the on-field momentum: the 2027 class checked in this week at No. 5 nationally, led by four-star wideout Melvin Matlock, four-star tackle Cole Schmitz, and three-star tight end Emanuel Guillermo.

Vegas and the model both lean Kentucky slightly (see the Dispatch's full betting breakdown on the front page), but this Arizona State team has made a habit of playing its best football at home. Beers, typically, isn't saying much about the line. He's just saying his team is ready.
