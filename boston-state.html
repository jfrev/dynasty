Bye Week in Riyadh: Boston State Takes Its 7-0 Season on the Road for an Aramco Partnership Trip

No. 20 in the country, undefeated, and now spending the bye on the other side of the world as part of a new sponsorship activation with Aramco.

By Priya Nakamura · The Liberty Ledger

Most bye weeks involve extra film sessions and a few days off. Boston State's program spent this one on a flight to Riyadh, part of a new sponsorship activation with Aramco that continues the pattern of Gulf investment underwriting this program's rise. The trip includes clinics, a stadium tour, and a series of promotional appearances for Kanoff and select players, all while the team sits at a corrected 7-0 (4-0 in the ACC) and No. 20 in the country.

Back home, the recruiting board keeps climbing right alongside the win total — Boston State's 2027 class checked in this week at No. 6 nationally, with four-star cornerback Fredrick Vaughns joining the previously reported commitments from quarterback Cliff Truman and wide receiver Mitch Britt.

Kanoff, typically media-shy about the financial side of the operation, kept his public comments about the trip strictly about football: rest, recovery, and staying sharp for the stretch run. The rest of the league, watching a two-year-old FBS program become a top-20 team with a top-10 class and an international sponsorship itinerary, is left to draw its own conclusions about what "new money" actually means at this level.
