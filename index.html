Week 8 · Upset Alert

Wisconsin Stuns No. 4 Miami, 38-21, in Dirk Diggler's Signature Win

A .500 team with three losses just knocked off a top-five program by three scores — the loudest result of the week, in either conference.

By the Dispatch Editorial Board · Week 8

Forget the 4-3 record Wisconsin carried into this week. The Badgers just beat the No. 4 team in the country by 17 points, taking down Miami 38-21 in what is, without much competition, the biggest result of the season so far. It's the signature win of Dirk Diggler's first year in Madison, and it arrives at a moment when this program's season looked like it was drifting toward irrelevance.

The formula was pure Diggler: control the line of scrimmage, lean on the run until the defense stops respecting anything else, and let a patient, physical brand of football wear down a more talented opponent over four quarters. Wisconsin controlled the clock for most of the game and put it away late with exactly the kind of drive this staff has been promising since fall camp.

Wisconsin moves to 5-3, 3-2 in the ACC. It won't be enough on its own to erase three losses from the record, but it's the kind of result that changes how a program is talked about for the rest of the year — and maybe how recruiters at Wisconsin's front door get answered from here on out.

Week 8 · Game Preview

No. 11 Kentucky Takes Its Unbeaten Record Into the Desert to Face a Red-Hot Arizona State

Two of the Big 12's best stories collide in Tempe this week — an undefeated Kentucky team built in the trenches against a 6-1 Arizona State squad that's arrived a year ahead of schedule.

By the Dispatch Editorial Board · Week 8

There is no bigger game left on this week's slate than Kentucky at Arizona State, and there may not be a bigger game on the whole Big 12 calendar this season. Kentucky arrives at 7-0, 4-0 in conference, ranked No. 11 in the country and riding the trenches-first identity LaSperm brought over from his Syracuse title team. Arizona State counters at 6-1, 4-1, unranked but arguably playing the best football of any team in the league not currently undefeated.

Both programs are led by first-year head coaches, which makes this as much a measuring stick for the coaches as the rosters. LaSperm has Kentucky playing the same disciplined, physical brand of football that won him a championship up north. Slammin Beers, the most unpredictable hire of the offseason, has Arizona State believing in a way that's been hard for outsiders to quantify — this program simply plays its best football at home, and Tempe on a Saturday night is as difficult an environment as the Big 12 offers.

The line reflects a genuinely close matchup on paper. But there's a factor that doesn't show up in any depth chart: Kentucky is being piloted by the most battle-tested decision-maker in this league, a player who has simply made fewer mistakes in big moments than anyone else at the controls this season. That's worth more than a field goal against a team still finding its footing at this level. The Dispatch likes Kentucky to survive a hostile crowd and leave Tempe still unbeaten.
