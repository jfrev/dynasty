Week 8 · Game Preview

No. 11 Kentucky Takes Its Unbeaten Record Into the Desert to Face a Red-Hot Arizona State

Two of the Big 12's best stories collide in Tempe this week — an undefeated Kentucky team built in the trenches against a 6-1 Arizona State squad that's arrived a year ahead of schedule.

By the Dispatch Editorial Board · Week 8

There is no bigger game on this week's slate than Kentucky at Arizona State, and there may not be a bigger game on the whole Big 12 calendar this season. Kentucky arrives at 7-0, 4-0 in conference, ranked No. 11 in the country and riding the trenches-first identity LaSperm brought over from his Syracuse title team. Arizona State counters at 6-1, 4-1, unranked but arguably playing the best football of any team in the league not currently undefeated.

Both programs are led by first-year head coaches, which makes this as much a measuring stick for the coaches as the rosters. LaSperm has Kentucky playing the same disciplined, physical brand of football that won him a championship up north. Slammin Beers, the most unpredictable hire of the offseason, has Arizona State believing in a way that's been hard for outsiders to quantify — this program simply plays its best football at home, and Tempe on a Saturday night is as difficult an environment as the Big 12 offers.

The line reflects a genuinely close matchup on paper. But there's a factor that doesn't show up in any depth chart: Kentucky is being piloted by the most battle-tested decision-maker in this league, a player who has simply made fewer mistakes in big moments than anyone else at the controls this season. That's worth more than a field goal against a team still finding its footing at this level. The Dispatch likes Kentucky to survive a hostile crowd and leave Tempe still unbeaten.
