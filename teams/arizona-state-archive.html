Nobody's Talking About the Sun Devils. Their Brand-New Head Coach Would Prefer It Stay That Way

Slammin Beers gets his first head coaching job in the desert, inheriting a roster that's better than its reputation.

By Nico Alvarado · The Desert Fork Report

Preseason polls are a popularity contest as much as anything else, and Arizona State has never been great at winning that part of the sport. This offseason adds a new twist: first-time head coach Slammin Beers, a name that raised eyebrows around the league as much as it did in recruiting circles.

Whatever else Beers is, he's not shy — camp reports describe a program that runs loud, physical practices and a locker room that seems to genuinely like playing for him. The roster he inherited looks nothing like an afterthought.

If there's a knock, it's that nobody has any real data on how Beers manages a season yet. We're finding out fast.
