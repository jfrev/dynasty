Bye Week Brings a Silver Lining: Florida State's Class Cracks the Top 40

Still 4-3, still on the bye, still stinging from the Thorne miss — but the Seminoles' 2027 class checked in at No. 35 nationally, headlined by a pair of four-star edge rushers.

By Dana Marchetti · The Renegade Report

Florida State's bye week hasn't changed the record — still 4-3, still 2-2 in the ACC — but it has brought some clarity on the recruiting side. The 2027 class checked in this week at No. 35 in the national rankings, built around a pair of four-star edge rushers in Xavier Heath and Alex Brewer, plus four-star middle linebacker Donte Louis.

It's a decent-not-great number for a program with Florida State's name and history, and it arrives in the same week the staff is still processing the loss of five-star quarterback James Thorne to Ole Miss. Guardiola's staff has been careful in front of the media not to overreact to either the ranking or the miss, pointing out there's a full month of the cycle left.

On the field, this bye is about self-scouting more than anything else. 4-3 with real talent on the roster is not the story anyone in this building wanted at the season's halfway point, and there's still half a season left to change it.
