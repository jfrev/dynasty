Week 8

A Bye Week to Regroup — and a Recruiting Gut-Punch to Answer For

At 4-3, Florida State finally gets a week off. It's arriving at the same moment the program lost its top quarterback target to Ole Miss.

By Dana Marchetti · The Renegade Report

Florida State's bye week was supposed to be about rest and self-scouting. Instead, it's being spent answering questions about James Thorne, the five-star quarterback who chose Ole Miss this week over the Seminoles and Boston State. Guardiola's staff had been in on Thorne since the spring, and losing him stings in a season that's already been more grind than glide.

On the field, 4-3 and 2-2 in the ACC is not the return-to-glory story anyone in this building was picturing back in August. The tempo offense Guardiola installed has flashed in stretches — explosive, hard to simulate in practice — but has also gone cold for entire quarters, and the defense has bent more than a roster this talented should allow.

There's still half a season to write a different ending. Guardiola has been here before, just on the other side of a championship. He knows what the second half of a season can undo, in either direction.

Preseason

Guardiola Comes Home — And Brings Championship Expectations With Him

The Seminoles didn't just keep their talent this offseason. They got their old coach back, fresh off a title at USC.

By Dana Marchetti · The Renegade Report

Fall camp closed the way it usually does at Florida State: with more questions about who plays too many positions well than who doesn't play any well enough. This year there's a new wrinkle — the man running the whole operation is Pipo Guardiola, back in Tallahassee for the first time since he left for USC, a national championship ring now in his pocket.

The homecoming storyline writes itself, but the roster underneath it is what actually has this building buzzing. The starting lineup reads like a preseason All-Conference ballot, and Guardiola's staff spent camp installing the same tempo-based system that got the best out of his USC offense.

Nobody in this building is talking about just making a bowl game. The bar is the same as it's always been in Tallahassee, and now the man who just cleared it somewhere else is the one trying to clear it here.
