Week 8

Unbeaten Meets Undefeated: No. 11 Kentucky Comes to Tempe

Arizona State is 6-1 and quietly one of the best stories in the Big 12. Now they host a Kentucky team that hasn't lost yet.

By Nico Alvarado · The Desert Fork Report

Slammin Beers wanted this game the moment the schedule came out. A first-time head coach with a 6-1 record and one league loss doesn't get many chances to make a signature statement, and hosting an undefeated, No. 11 Kentucky team in Tempe is about as loud a stage as this program gets this year.

The Sun Devils have won with the same formula all year — a physical run game and a secondary that's finally deep enough to match speed with speed — and Beers has stuck to it even after the one loss earlier this season. Kentucky's offensive and defensive lines present the toughest matchup of that identity yet.

Vegas and the model both lean Kentucky slightly (see the Dispatch's full betting breakdown on the front page), but this Arizona State team has made a habit of playing its best football at home. Beers, typically, isn't saying much about the line. He's just saying his team is ready.

Nobody's Talking About the Sun Devils. Their Brand-New Head Coach Would Prefer It Stay That Way

Slammin Beers gets his first head coaching job in the desert, inheriting a roster that's better than its reputation.

By Nico Alvarado · The Desert Fork Report

Preseason polls are a popularity contest as much as anything else, and Arizona State has never been great at winning that part of the sport. This offseason adds a new twist: first-time head coach Slammin Beers, a name that raised eyebrows around the league as much as it did in recruiting circles.

Whatever else Beers is, he's not shy — camp reports describe a program that runs loud, physical practices and a locker room that seems to genuinely like playing for him. The roster he inherited looks nothing like an afterthought.

If there's a knock, it's that nobody has any real data on how Beers manages a season yet. We're finding out fast.
