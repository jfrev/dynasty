No. 19 and Idle: Army's Recruiting Class Lands at No. 33 During the Bye

A first-year coach with a complicated history has the Black Knights ranked and 5-1. The bye week brought good news on the trail, too, headlined by a five-star.

By Colonel Pete Renner (Ret.) · The Long Gray Line Dispatch

Army's bye week has been quiet on the scoreboard and loud on the recruiting trail. The 2027 class checked in at No. 33 nationally this week, and the headline is a name that doesn't usually show up on an Army commitment list: five-star running back Emanuel Bullock, joined by four-star linebacker Shaquille Lampkin and four-star cornerback Charlie Easy.

On the field, it's still 5-1, 4-1 in a brand-new Big 12 home, and still No. 19 in the country — the highest ranking the program has carried in years. Da'Rock has stayed characteristically unbothered by the attention, keeping the meeting room focused on tape review rather than the poll.

Landing a five-star at Army has always been the exception, not the rule. Getting one to commit in the same season the program is ranked for the first time in years is not a coincidence Da'Rock's staff is shy about pointing to.
