Kentucky's New Coach Brings a Championship Blueprint — and a Reworked Trenches-First Identity

LaSperm arrives from Syracuse with a title on his résumé and a plan to win the Big 12 the same way he won up north: in the trenches.

By Callie Broussard · The Bluegrass Blitz

There is no easy schedule in the Big 12, and Kentucky's new head coach isn't pretending otherwise. LaSperm arrives from Syracuse, where he built a national champion on the strength of a physical offensive and defensive line, and the plan at Kentucky is a direct copy-paste.

The quarterback room is the biggest variable under the new staff, but LaSperm has said publicly he's comfortable letting the offense grow into itself.

Nobody outside the building expects a run at the top of the standings in year one under a new coach. Nobody inside it is ruling it out, either.
