No. 8 No More: Rhode Island Falls at Houston, 27-24, in Its Toughest Test Yet

The program's first-ever national ranking survived seven weeks. It didn't survive a three-point road loss to Houston.

By Tomas Reyes · The Ocean State Outlook

Rhode Island's first-ever national ranking lasted exactly as long as it took to play its toughest game of the season. The Ghost Bats fell at Houston, 27-24, a game that came down to the final possession and will almost certainly knock the program out of this week's poll after entering at No. 8.

Tanka Jahari's team did what it's done all year — competed with anyone, played with more discipline than its recruiting rankings would suggest — but a handful of missed opportunities in Houston proved costly against a team that made fewer mistakes down the stretch. The locker room's reaction, by all accounts, was disappointment rather than panic; this is still a first-time head coach's first season, and a three-point road loss to a quality opponent isn't the kind of result that changes the plan.

There was better news on the trail: Rhode Island's 2027 class checked in this week at No. 58 nationally, with four-star tackle Manny Kalis, four-star running back James Raines, and four-star defensive tackle Jose Martino all in the fold. It's the kind of class that suggests this season's surprising run isn't a one-year fluke, ranking or no ranking.
