No. 8 Rhode Island: A Ranking Nobody Saw Coming Faces Its Next Test in Houston

Tanka Jahari's first season has the Ghost Bats ranked in the top ten. A matchup with Houston is the next chance to prove it's not a fluke.

By Tomas Reyes · The Ocean State Outlook

Nobody picked Rhode Island to be a top-ten team in year one under a first-time head coach, least of all the coach himself — Tanka Jahari has spent all season deflecting questions about rankings and talking about "building week to week" instead. At 5-2 and No. 8 in the country, the building is starting to look a lot like a finished product.

The formula has been effort and detail over name recognition, exactly as advertised in camp, but the results have exceeded even the most optimistic projections inside the building. This week's matchup with Houston is the kind of game a team either backs up its ranking in or gets exposed by, and the Ghost Bats know it.

Jahari's answer to all of it has been consistent all year: prepare the same way regardless of the number next to the team name. For a program that had never sniffed a national ranking before this season, that discipline might be exactly why it's there in the first place.

Preseason

Rhode Island Enters the Big 12 With a New Coach and Something to Prove

Tanka Jahari gets his first head coaching opportunity as the Ghost Bats settle into a new conference and a new level of competition.

By Tomas Reyes · The Ocean State Outlook

Ask the coaching staff what Rhode Island is going to be known for this season and you'll get an answer about effort and detail before anything about scheme or system. That's typical of a program stepping into a bigger conference for the first time, with first-time head coach Tanka Jahari building the identity from the ground up.

The roster construction this offseason leaned on competitiveness over name recognition. It's a harder sell in recruiting against the league's bigger spenders, but it's also produced a locker room that, by all early accounts, is unusually tight.

Nobody was picking Rhode Island to contend for the Big 12 in year one. Jahari didn't seem interested in that conversation anyway.
