Week 8

No. 11 Kentucky Takes Its Perfect Record on the Road to Arizona State

LaSperm's first season is off to a 7-0 start. A trip to face a 6-1 Arizona State team is the toughest test yet.

By Callie Broussard · The Bluegrass Blitz

Seven weeks into LaSperm's first season, the copy-paste from his Syracuse blueprint is working almost exactly as advertised: control the trenches, lean on defense, let the record speak. It's spoken loudly enough to get Kentucky to 7-0, 4-0 in the Big 12, and into the rankings at No. 11.

This week is the toughest gut check of the season so far — a true road environment against an Arizona State team that's just as hot, sitting at 6-1 under its own first-year coach. LaSperm has downplayed the "unbeaten showdown" framing all week in meetings, sticking to his usual message about stacking one good week on another.

The Dispatch has Kentucky as a slight road favorite (full breakdown on the front page), for reasons that go a little beyond the depth chart. Whatever the number says, this is the first time all year Kentucky's perfect record is actually in real jeopardy.

Preseason

Kentucky's New Coach Brings a Championship Blueprint — and a Reworked Trenches-First Identity

LaSperm arrives from Syracuse with a title on his résumé and a plan to win the Big 12 the same way he won up north: in the trenches.

By Callie Broussard · The Bluegrass Blitz

There is no easy schedule in the Big 12, and Kentucky's new head coach isn't pretending otherwise. LaSperm arrives from Syracuse, where he built a national champion on the strength of a physical offensive and defensive line, and the plan at Kentucky is a direct copy-paste.

The quarterback room is the biggest variable under the new staff, but LaSperm has said publicly he's comfortable letting the offense grow into itself.

Nobody outside the building expects a run at the top of the standings in year one under a new coach. Nobody inside it is ruling it out, either.
