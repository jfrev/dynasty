No. 11 Kentucky Takes Its Perfect Record on the Road to Arizona State — With a Top-10 Class Behind It

LaSperm's first season is off to a 7-0 start, and now the No. 7 recruiting class in the country. A trip to face a 6-1 Arizona State team is the toughest test yet.

By Callie Broussard · The Bluegrass Blitz

Seven weeks into LaSperm's first season, the copy-paste from his Syracuse blueprint is working almost exactly as advertised: control the trenches, lean on defense, let the record speak. It's spoken loudly enough to get Kentucky to 7-0, 4-0 in the Big 12, and into the rankings at No. 11 — and now the trail is following the wins, with the 2027 class checking in at No. 7 nationally behind four-star tackle Max Mauch, four-star center Sean Busby, and three-star guard Cam Levrone.

This week is the toughest gut check of the season so far — a true road environment against an Arizona State team that's just as hot, sitting at 6-1 under its own first-year coach. LaSperm has downplayed the "unbeaten showdown" framing all week in meetings, sticking to his usual message about stacking one good week on another.

The Dispatch has Kentucky as a slight road favorite (full breakdown on the front page), for reasons that go a little beyond the depth chart. Whatever the number says, this is the first time all year Kentucky's perfect record is actually in real jeopardy.
