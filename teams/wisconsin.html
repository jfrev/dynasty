Week 8

A .500 Team Looks for Its Footing Against Miami

Wisconsin sits at 4-3 in Dirk Diggler's first season. A home date with Miami is a chance to build some momentum into the stretch run.

By Erik Solheim · The Camp Randall Chronicle

Four wins, three losses, and a locker room that insists the record doesn't reflect how competitive most of those three losses actually were — that's where Wisconsin sits heading into a Week 8 matchup with Miami. Dirk Diggler's first season in Madison has looked exactly like what a coaching change usually looks like: the identity is there, the results are still catching up.

The offensive line remains the strength of this roster, and Diggler has leaned on it the way he did at Auburn and Kent State — patient runs, play-action off of it, and a defense that's allowed this team to stay in every game it's played. What's been missing is finishing: two of the three losses came down to the fourth quarter.

Miami presents a chance to flip that script with a signature ACC win. Diggler has talked all week about execution over new wrinkles. In Madison, that's usually the right answer.

Preseason

New Coach, Same Blueprint: Dirk Diggler Inherits Another Load-Bearing Offensive Line

Wisconsin's new head coach spent his career at Kent State and Auburn. In Madison, he's found a roster built exactly the way he likes to coach.

By Erik Solheim · The Camp Randall Chronicle

You could set your watch by a Wisconsin preseason storyline: a new running back nobody outside the state has heard of yet, an offensive line that returns most of a group already considered one of the best in the league, and — new this year — a head coach who's spent his whole career elsewhere finally getting a roster built for exactly his style.

Dirk Diggler made his name at Kent State and later Auburn running a physical, patient offense, and Wisconsin's personnel might be the best fit he's ever inherited.

Don't expect fireworks in August. Expect Diggler's Badgers to still be playing their brand of football in the fourth quarter of every game on the schedule.
