Week 8

Undefeated, Ranked, and Still Recruiting Like It: Inside Boston State's Biggest Week Yet

No. 20 in the country at 8-0. Seven new four-star commitments in a single week. The fastest rise in the sport just keeps accelerating.

By Priya Nakamura · The Boston State Beat

Boston State entered its bye week undefeated, ranked for the first time in program history at No. 20, and — as if to underline the point — closed out the week by landing seven four-star commitments in a single recruiting cycle. The headline names are quarterback Cliff Truman and wide receiver Mitch Britt, but the full haul reads like a program that has nothing left to prove to recruits about whether the money is real.

It also landed a much smaller consolation story this week: five-star quarterback James Thorne, a target Boston State and Florida State had both pursued hard, chose Ole Miss instead. Kanoff's staff didn't sound particularly rattled by it — with Truman already in the fold and Zion Marchetti still developing on the current roster, the quarterback room here is not exactly bare.

What's undeniable is the trajectory. Two years removed from FCS football, Kingdom Holding Company's investment has produced an undefeated, ranked ACC program with one of the best recruiting classes in the country in the same week. The rest of the league can keep calling it new money. At 8-0, it's just money that wins.

Preseason

From FCS to the ACC in Two Years: Inside Boston State's Fast, Expensive Rise

Kingdom Holding Company's investment turned a mid-major into an ACC program with a back-to-back national champion on the sideline. The rest of the league is still adjusting to it.

By Priya Nakamura · The Boston State Beat

Two years ago, Boston State was playing FCS football in front of a few thousand people. Today, they're an ACC program with a payroll and a facilities budget that rival programs a century older, and the fastest rise of any team in the country has a name behind it: Kingdom Holding Company, the Saudi-based investment firm that bankrolled the jump from FCS to FBS, funded the push into the ACC, and wrote the checks that got both a roster overhaul and Jack Kanoff's signature on a contract.

Kanoff, fresh off back-to-back national championships at Midwestern University, is the headline of the whole operation.

The skepticism from the rest of the league is real and out loud. Kanoff's answer, so far, has been the same one he gave at Midwestern: let the record speak.
